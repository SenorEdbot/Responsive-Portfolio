Eddie Franco

Hello! My name is Eddie, I currently live in Lawrence, KS after living in Denver for 3 years. I have a 1-bedroom apartment with Allie, my girlfriend. I was born in Garden City, KS and raised in Salina, KS by my parents who still live there with my 2 sisters, Michelle, 21, and Lizzy, 15. After receiving my high school diploma from Salina South High School I went to school for 2 years at Kansas Wesleyan University. I was attending University on a soccer and academic scholarship where I studied Computer Science for 2 years.

I had an opportunity to move to Denver, CO to ensure I could be closer to the job market upon completing school. My credits from Kansas Wesleyan did not transfer properly and would have required an additional 4 years of school to complete my Bachelor of Computer Science at any other University, even though I had 59 current credits. During this time I worked for a Moving Company. Starting as a labor employee and working my way up to supervisor I was exposed to the Payroll Process as well as the Scheduling. I was able to build spreadsheets and forms to complete essential functions that were being doing by hand for my boss.

These 'beginner' spreadsheets as I would call them in comparison to the current system I've written for Black Belt Movers were things such as an Hours Log and a Clock In/Out System for the movers. My boss' boss saw the new spreadsheets and how things were being done and requested I work in the office as a Reservationist and work into an IT role as I proved a need for one.

That was 2 years ago when I started in the 'corporate office' as it is referred to at Black Belt. Since then I have updated the entire system to be completely paperless and operating solely through Google Apps Suite. This includes the 'Booking Ticket' form that is filled out anytime a phone call is taken, a Time Clock System for the Office, monitoring the build and maintaining a WordPress website, as well as automated Reconcile Spreadsheets that pull data from 10+ separate spreadsheets.

I am looking forward to being able to market myself as well as increase my skillset 100 times over. "If I know one thing it is that I know nothing", has been a piece of advise I try to take to heart when it comes to programming. I look forward to having a career where programming is my entire focus, rather than multi-tasking multiple roles in a smaller corporate office setting.
